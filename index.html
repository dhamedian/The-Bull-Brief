Word of the Day
Yield Curve
noun / economics
A graph showing interest rates on US government bonds at different maturity lengths. When short-term rates are higher than long-term ones (inverted), it has historically predicted recessions.
bonds interest rates recession signal Federal Reserve
Used in context
"Analysts pointed to the inverted yield curve as a warning sign, noting that short-term Treasury bills were paying more than 10-year bonds — a pattern that preceded each of the last seven US recessions."
